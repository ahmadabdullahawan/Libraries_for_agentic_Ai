The Evolution of Artificial Intelligence
Artificial intelligence (AI) has transformed from a niche academic field into a cornerstone of modern technology. At the forefront of this revolution 
are Large Language Models (LLMs), which have reshaped how machines understand and generate human
language. Unlike early rule-based systems, modern AI leverages deep neural networks trained on vast
ceans of internet data. This allows systems to grasp context, nuance, and even complex reasoning.
Natural Language Processing and Neural NetworksNatural Language Processing (NLP) bridges the gap between human communication and computer understanding.
Through the use of transformer architectures, AI models can process billions of parameters simultaneously.
These transformers utilize self-attention mechanisms, allowing the model to weigh the importance of different words in a sentence relative to one another.
Consequently, the AI can maintain the context of a long paragraph rather than evaluating words in isolation.
The Mechanism of TokenizationBefore an AI can process text, the text must be broken down into smaller, digestible pieces called tokens.
A token can be a whole word, a sub-word, or even a single character. For instance, the word "artificial" might be split into "art", "i", "fic", and "ial". 
This tokenization process converts raw text into numerical vectors that the neural network can manipulate mathematically, such as calculating distances of $10 × 10 ÷ 2$.
The Necessity of Data ChunkingWhen building AI applications like chatbots or proprietary search engines, feeding an entire book or massive document into the model all at once is impractical. 
This is primarily due to strict operational limitations, such as maximum context window sizes and high computational costs. To overcome this, developers rely on chunking. 
Chunking is the process of splitting a large document into smaller, manageable, and semantically coherent segments (chunks).
How Overlapping WorksIf documents are chunked blindly (e.g., stopping exactly at a character count), critical context can be fractured, leaving the AI unable to understand information that spans across the break point. 
To solve this, developers implement overlapping chunks. By allowing consecutive chunks to share a portion of text, the system ensures contextual continuity.
